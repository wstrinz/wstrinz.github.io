🌍 Iran War — Global View

How the world sees it · Oil exposure · Iran's PR problem · Atlantic Council · EUISS · Mar 2026

🗺️ Regional Stances on US-Israeli Action
🇮🇱 Israel & Gulf Arab States
✅ Supportive (Quietly)
Saudi Arabia, UAE quietly relieved — Iran has been attacking them too. Gulf states won't say it publicly but aren't upset Khamenei is gone. Israel is an active participant.
🇬🇧 UK / 🇨🇦 Canada / 🇦🇷 Argentina
↗ Cautiously Aligned
UK "trying to stay out of it" but quietly aligned with allies. Canada calibrated + aligned. Argentina supportive — has its own terrorism grievances against Iran (1994 AMIA bombing).
🇺🇦 Ukraine & Eastern Europe
↗ Schadenfreude + Concern
Ukraine quietly pleased Iran's drone capacity is being degraded — same drones hitting Ukrainian cities. But worried about precedent and US attention split.
🇨🇳 China
➡ Restrained / Stuck
"Characteristically limp" response — formulaic condemnation of US/Israel. In an impossible bind: Iran is their anti-Western ally AND the oil disruption is hammering their economy. Can't help Iran militarily, won't.
🇷🇺 Russia
➡ Not Riding to the Rescue
Condemned the strikes but doing nothing. Bogged down in Ukraine. Limited capacity and appetite to intervene. Iran is on its own strategically.
🇪🇺 EU (Divided)
↙ Split, Leaning Skeptical
No unity. Eastern members more supportive, France/Germany skeptical, Spain openly defiant. EU wants to defend rules-based order but won't follow US into this. Emergency energy meetings ongoing.
🌍 Global South / BRICS
↙ Skeptical of US
Default anti-Western framing. But Iran attacking commercial ships has alienated even some Global South countries that depend on Gulf shipping lanes. Not sympathy for Iran — more "both sides" energy.
🇪🇸 Spain / Turkey / Some Arab states
❌ Actively Opposed
Spain refusing US use of joint military bases, driven by domestic politics. Turkey vocally opposed. Some Arab public opinion deeply anti-Israel regardless of regime position.

💀 Iran's PR Problem — Why Attacking Everyone Isn't Working
Commercial Shipping Attacks
Hitting cargo ships from non-belligerent nations. China, India, Japan all importing through these lanes. Making enemies of the neutral observers who depend on Gulf trade.
US Embassy (Saudi Arabia)
Striking US infrastructure in Saudi Arabia pulls Riyadh closer to Washington. Saudi Arabia was trying to stay neutral. That position is harder to maintain now.
Kuwait / Regional Strikes
6 US soldiers killed in Kuwait. But Kuwait is an Arab country with its own public. Iran targeting it damages regional solidarity that might otherwise favor Tehran's anti-US framing.
Oil Disruption
The countries most hurt by Hormuz closure are China, India, Japan, South Korea — not the US. Iran is economically hurting its own potential supporters.
Lebanon / Hezbollah
Opening a second front with Israel in Lebanon. Lebanese civilians pay the cost. Hezbollah's "open war" declaration alienates Lebanese moderates who blame Iran for their country being a battlefield.
Rejection of Any Negotiation
Pezeshkian rejecting "unconditional surrender" is understandable, but Iran hasn't offered any alternative. "We won't talk" doesn't generate international sympathy for your position.
