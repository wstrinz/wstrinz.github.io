🇫🇷 Macron & Africa — Is the Hate Justified?

Specifically the Sahel · Honest assessment, receipts included · Not just vibes

🧾 The Receipts — Legitimate Grievances
Operation Barkhane: 9 Years, Net Negative
France's Sahel counterterrorism mission (2014–2022). Cost: 55+ French soldiers dead, ~$1B+/year. Result: Jihadist-controlled territory expanded during the operation. France's own senators called it a failure. The Sahel now accounts for over 50% of global terrorism deaths.
Expelled From 4 Countries — That's a Verdict
Mali (2022), Burkina Faso (2023), Niger (2023), CAR (2023) all expelled French forces and/or ambassadors. That's not just politics — four countries choosing Russian mercenaries over French partners is a damning popular verdict on what France delivered.
CFA Franc Reform Was Theater
Macron announced major reform of the CFA franc (Africa's colonial-era currency pegged to the euro). The "eco" rebrand required African countries to still deposit 50% of foreign reserves in France's treasury. Critics: same dependency, new name. The structural extraction continues.
Niger Uranium — 35 Years, Zero Development
Niger supplies ~15% of EU's uranium. Niger remains one of the world's poorest countries (bottom 3 HDI). French state company Orano (formerly Areva) extracted billions while Niger's people have no electricity in 85% of the country. The math on "partnership" doesn't work.
💬 Macron Said What? — His Greatest Hits
"One of the main reasons for Africa's difficulties is that... a woman can have 7 or 8 children."
— Macron, G20 Hamburg, July 2017. Framing African poverty as an African behavioral problem, not a structural one.
"There is something to change in African leaders' mentality... a certain ingratitude."
— Macron, January 2025, after the Sahel expulsions. Called expelled countries "ungrateful" rather than examining France's failure.
Dismissed France being "expelled" from Mali, saying troops left voluntarily.
— Macron, 2025. The Malian junta explicitly told France to leave. This is what "not taking responsibility" looks like.
"There are several countries in Africa that were never colonized and that are today among the most failed states."
— Macron, 2023. A reach. Ethiopia was only briefly occupied; it has had its own severe conflicts, but using it to justify colonialism is a lazy deflection.

⚖️ The Honest Complication (Don't Skip This)
It Got WORSE After France Left
The countries that expelled France are now in humanitarian catastrophe. Islamist fatalities hit record highs in 2024. Wagner didn't fix it — they made it worse for civilians (documented massacres). Tanvir's point about France failing is correct. But France leaving also didn't help the people living there.
The Jihadist Threat Was Real
JNIM and ISGS were genuinely expanding, killing tens of thousands of Sahelians before France intervened. Barkhane failed to stop them, but the threat wasn't invented. The failure was in strategy and colonial dynamics — not in the premise that there was a security problem.
The CFA Franc Predates Macron
This colonial currency system has existed since 1945. Macron inherited it and did attempt (weakly) to reform it. Legitimate to criticize how he handled it; harder to make it primarily his failure. France's structural extraction from Africa spans 15+ presidencies.
He Did Say Some Honest Things
Macron gave more direct speeches acknowledging French colonialism than any previous French president. Actions didn't match words. But "words without action" is different from denial. He also returned some African artifacts — small gesture, but real.

✅ Where You're Right
  • The "7 or 8 children" comment was genuinely inexcusable — paternalism dressed as policy
  • Barkhane failed on its own terms. Territory gained by jihadists, not lost
  • Getting expelled from 4 countries is an unprecedented diplomatic humiliation — and it happened on his watch
  • CFA franc reform was cosmetic — dependency structure intact
  • Calling expelled countries "ungrateful" instead of examining France's failures is exactly the problem
  • Niger uranium extraction with zero development benefit is a textbook neocolonial relationship
⚠️ Where It Gets Complicated
  • The Sahel is in worse shape now than when France was there — not Macron's doing alone
  • The structural extraction from Africa is a French institutional problem, not a Macron innovation
  • The jihadist threat was real; the question is whether intervention helped or hurt
  • "Worst" implies there are better alternatives — who? The Sahel's current Russian-backed juntas are massacring their own people
  • He acknowledged colonialism more explicitly than his predecessors (words matter even when actions lag)
Bottom line: Macron is a legitimate target for criticism on Africa — especially the condescending rhetoric, the Barkhane failure, and the CFA franc theater. The specific frustration Sahelians feel is earned. But "the worst" is doing extra work. France's Africa problem is systemic and decades-deep. Macron managed it poorly and arrogantly. He didn't invent it, and the people who expelled him haven't found a better replacement. Your hate is about 65% justified. The remaining 35% is really about France as a whole, not Macron specifically.
