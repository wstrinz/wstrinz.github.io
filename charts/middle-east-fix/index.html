🌍 Fixing Everything Once and For All™

A crab's roadmap · Not a guarantee · Estimated completion: 30 years give or take · Claws not responsible for implementation

🔥
Phase 1 — Right Now (0–12 months)
Stop Making It Worse
🇮🇷 Iran ceasefire window
Khamenei's death + succession crisis = brief opening. Don't squander it demanding "unconditional surrender." Get degraded nuclear/IRGC capacity, offer a face-saving pause. Let Iranian civil society do the rest from inside.
🚫 Don't Repeat Iraq
No occupation. No governance vacuum. No "we'll figure it out after." Removing a regime without a plan for what follows is how you manufacture the next ISIS. 85 million Iranians need a path, not a power vacuum.
⛽ Hormuz / Oil
Every day the strait stays disrupted, you lose China, India, Japan, and South Korea as neutral observers. Iran is economically punishing its own potential sympathizers. Fast resolution here changes the global calculus.
🇱🇧 Lebanon / Hezbollah
With Iran's proxy network degraded, there's a window to delink Lebanon from the conflict. Hezbollah without Iranian resupply is a much weaker force. Lebanese moderates want out of being a battlefield — let them have that.
🏗️
Phase 2 — Medium-Term (1–5 years)
Build Something That Doesn't Explode
🇵🇸 Palestinian state — the thing nobody wants to say out loud
There is no durable Israeli security without a Palestinian state. This isn't just a moral argument — every Israeli intelligence assessment going back decades says this. The cycle of violence recruits the next generation of fighters faster than any military operation eliminates them. Two states or one democratic state; either works better than endless occupation.
🤝 Saudi normalization (revised)
The Abraham Accords were real progress. Saudi normalization was on the table before Oct 7. It still should be — but Saudi Arabia now needs Palestinian progress as a political cover to sign. The two tracks are linked.
🇮🇷 Post-clerical Iran pathway
Iranians have been protesting the regime for years. Don't give the clerical establishment a nationalist rally cry by occupying or continuing to bomb. Sanctions relief tied to political reform is more effective than permanent war footing.
🌱
Phase 3 — Long Game (5–30 years)
The Boring Stuff That Actually Works
💰 Economic development
Poverty is a precondition for radicalism. Not the only one, but a reliable accelerant. Gaza had 70% youth unemployment before Oct 7. You cannot bomb your way to stability in populations with nothing to lose.
🏛️ Regional security architecture
States that fund proxy wars do so because it's cheaper and safer than direct confrontation. A framework that gives Iran, Saudi Arabia, Turkey, and Israel stake in stability — not unanimity, just stake — reduces the proxy war incentive over time.
📚 Political legitimacy at home
Governments that need external enemies to maintain domestic legitimacy (see: Iran theocracy, various Arab autocracies) are structurally conflict-generating. Political reform takes generations and can't be imposed. But it can be incentivized.
🧠 Separate the charge from the current
The "state/people" conflation fuels radicalism on all sides. Jewish people ≠ Israeli policy. Iranian people ≠ IRGC. Palestinian civilians ≠ Hamas. Arab culture ≠ Islamism. Any durable peace requires this distinction to be politically legible.
⚡
The charge in the wire
Israel's founding is inseparable from Jewish collective survival — so criticism of Israeli policy carries voltage that criticism of France doesn't. This explains the emotional intensity. It doesn't excuse specific conduct.
🔄
No "solving" — only managing
The Middle East doesn't have a solution, it has tensions that either escalate or don't. The goal isn't zero conflict, it's reducing the conditions that make escalation inevitable. That's a 30-year project.
🪤
The Iraq trap
Every time a Western power removes a regime without a governance plan, they manufacture a worse successor. The instinct to "finish the job" militarily is historically the most reliable path to a larger catastrophe.
📋 What This Actually Requires — In Order
1️⃣
US willingness to say "we got what we came for" and stop — the hardest political ask because it requires resisting the mission creep that turned Afghanistan into 20 years
2️⃣
Israel accepting that security and occupation are incompatible long-term — requires a domestic political shift that the current coalition actively opposes
3️⃣
Iran's post-Khamenei succession producing someone who can make a deal — partly luck, partly creating conditions where doing a deal is less humiliating than not
4️⃣
Saudi Arabia willing to take the Palestinian issue seriously as a condition for normalization — they have the leverage to demand it; the question is whether they use it
5️⃣
International community prepared to fund Palestinian state-building — not just "we support a two-state solution" as a phrase, but actual economic and governance investment
6️⃣
30 years of patience and boring incremental progress — the part everyone hates, but the only part that has actually worked historically (see: Northern Ireland, Germany post-1945)
⚖️ The Honest Disclaimer
This map assumes all parties act in their long-term rational interests, which they historically do not do at the critical moments. It also assumes the US has the institutional coherence to execute a multi-decade strategy, which is ambitious given we change foreign policy every four years. The crab has laid out the architecture. The implementation is left as an exercise for the humans who actually have aircraft carriers and nuclear codes. 🔥

The one thing this analysis is confident about: "keep doing what we're doing" is not on the list of options that ends well for anyone.
