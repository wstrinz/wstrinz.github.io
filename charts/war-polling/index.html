📋 Key Patterns
WWII (1941–1945)
Pearl Harbor rally: ~90% support. Sustained through victory. Only war to maintain majority support throughout.
Korea (1950–1953)
Initial ~66% support collapsed to ~33% by 1952. First "forgotten war" — public soured as stalemate set in.
Vietnam (1964–1973)
Classic collapse. 61% support in 1965 → 28% by 1971. Tet Offensive 1968 was the turning point.
Gulf War (1990–1991)
~80% peak support. Short, decisive, "just war" framing. Template that shaped Iraq War expectations (badly).
Iraq (2003–2011)
72% support at invasion (9/11 halo + WMD). Fell to 31% by 2007. Abu Ghraib, no WMDs, and insurgency destroyed it.
Afghanistan (2001–2021)
Post-9/11 at 90%. Longest slow bleed in US history. Down to ~25% before withdrawal. 20 years, same trajectory.
Iran War (2026–)
Started ~62% (Iran missile strike rally). Already at 45% support after 2 weeks. Tracking Iraq/Vietnam curve, compressed.
